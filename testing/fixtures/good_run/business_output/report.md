# Hormuz closure: price impact and relief options

## Snapshot

A closure or partial disruption of the Strait of Hormuz would drive a sharp crude price spike on an immediate risk premium [s01], already visible in jumping tanker freight rates [s04]. Relief levers exist but are partial and lagged.

## Evidence highlights

- OPEC+ spare capacity is estimated near 4 mb/d, offering a partial supply offset [s02].
- US SPR drawdown is material but constrained by drawdown mechanics and logistics timing [s03].
- The Goreh-Jask bypass cannot replace full Hormuz throughput [s05].
- Official strait-security statements add headline volatility [s06].
- Combined freight and risk-premium signals point to near-term tightening [s01, s04].
- <NewsCard source-id="s03"/> SPR timing is the key swing variable for relief.

## How news reshapes the working thesis

The thesis is supported: a short-term spike is likely, with relief depending on coordinated SPR releases [s03] and OPEC spare-capacity activation [s02].

## Risks & blind spots

- Multilateral coordination assumptions are unproven.
- Bypass pipeline limits may be understated [s05].
