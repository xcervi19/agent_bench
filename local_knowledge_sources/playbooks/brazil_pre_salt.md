# Brazil pre-salt

**Commodity:** crude  
**Geography:** Brazil — Santos / Campos pre-salt  
**Last reviewed:** 2026-07-23  

## Executive summary

Brazil’s pre-salt (Búzios/Tupi class) is a structural Atlantic supply growth story steered by Petrobras policy and offshore logistics. Scan must catch first: petrobras.com.br production/FPSO updates, ANTAQ maritime logistics, weather (INMET) for offshore ops, and BRL/macro (BCB) as CAPEX/cost context.

## Price drivers (trader lens)

1. FPSO start-ups, maintenance, and output guidance (Petrobras).
2. Politically driven pricing/investment policy shifts.
3. Offshore weather and maritime restrictions (ANTAQ/INMET).
4. Atlantic Basin light/medium grade balances into China/Europe.
5. BRL and domestic fuel policy affecting Petrobras incentives.

## Infrastructure & logistics

Pre-salt hubs in Santos/Campos basins load via FPSOs and shuttle/export systems into Atlantic routes toward Asia (Cape/Panama-sensitive) and Europe/USGC. Maintenance seasons and FPSO ramp schedules dominate medium-term supply.

## Geopolitical triggers

- Petrobras leadership/policy shock on pricing or CAPEX.
- Major FPSO delay or unplanned shutdown.
- Domestic fuel-policy intervention changing export incentive.
- Extreme South Atlantic weather interrupting loadings.
- China demand shock altering Brazil grade differentials.
