# Red Sea / Bab el-Mandeb

**Commodity:** crude | LNG | products  
**Geography:** Red Sea — Bab el-Mandeb — approaches to Suez  
**Last reviewed:** 2026-07-23  

## Executive summary

Attacks and advisories in the southern Red Sea / Bab el-Mandeb force Cape diversions, lengthening Asia–Europe voyages and lifting freight and delayed LNG/crude supply. Scan must catch first: UKMTO incidents, Suez Canal Authority transit changes, Royal Navy / regional naval posture, and Port of Djibouti / Red Sea weather ops context; escalate with yemen_houthi_red_sea for conflict detail.

## Price drivers (trader lens)

1. Confirmed attacks or UKMTO warnings altering transit willingness.
2. Suez tonnage / transit fee or suspension signals (SCA).
3. Vessel diversion around Cape (voyage days → delayed arrivals, freight).
4. War-risk insurance and liner/tanker avoidance of southern Red Sea.
5. Europe-bound LNG/crude timing gaps vs Atlantic basin alternatives.

## Infrastructure & logistics

Bab el-Mandeb gates the southern Red Sea into the Suez lane; diversions use Cape of Good Hope (see cape_route_saldanha). Europe–Asia container and energy flows share the corridor — energy impact is delayed cargoes and freight, not only spot barrel loss. Djibouti sits on the approach; Yanbu is a major Red Sea crude outlet.

## Geopolitical triggers

- Missile/drone/boat attack on tanker or boxship confirmed via UKMTO.
- SCA advisory reducing or suspending transit.
- Major liner/tanker companies announcing indefinite Red Sea avoidance (confirm via ops + UKMTO, not social only).
- Escalation involving shore targets in Yemen or wider Red Sea states (see yemen_houthi_red_sea).
- Naval coalition posture change (Royal Navy and partners).
