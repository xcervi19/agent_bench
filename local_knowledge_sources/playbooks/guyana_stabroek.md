# Guyana Stabroek

**Commodity:** crude  
**Geography:** Guyana — Stabroek Block / offshore Atlantic  
**Last reviewed:** 2026-07-23  

## Executive summary

Guyana’s Stabroek-driven offshore crude is a fast-growing Atlantic sweet supply stream; political, fiscal, and maritime security noise can still move differentials. Scan must catch first: GGMC and EPA Guyana regulatory signals, GRA/fiscal context, GDF Coast Guard / MARAD maritime advisories, and realized export continuity.

## Price drivers (trader lens)

1. FPSO ramp schedule and unplanned offshore outages.
2. Regulatory/environmental actions (EPA/GGMC) delaying wells or loadings.
3. Maritime security / advisory changes (GDF, US MARAD).
4. Fiscal/political disputes affecting operator investment pace.
5. Atlantic sweet competition with USGC/Nigeria/North Sea grades.

## Infrastructure & logistics

Deepwater Stabroek FPSO hub loads Atlantic-bound sweet crude. Neighboring maritime boundary politics and offshore logistics are operational risks. Competes in Atlantic Basin sweet pool with USGC and WAF grades.

## Geopolitical triggers

- Border/maritime dispute flare with neighbors.
- EPA stop-work or major spill response.
- Fiscal renegotiation headlines confirmed via GRA/GGMC.
- MARAD or Coast Guard advisory elevating war-risk/ops cost.
- FPSO turnaround slipping peak-season loadings.
